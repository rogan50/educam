Robotics and Embedded Systems

Meaning of Robotics

Robotics is the branch of technology that deals with the design, constructions, operations, and use of robots.

A Robot is a machine that can be programmed to perform tasks automatically - either under human control (manual) or independently (autonomous.)

Definition

Robotic is the study and creation of robots that can carry out tasks automatically or with minimal human help.

What is a Robot?

A robot is an intelligent, computer-controlled machine that can:

  • Sense its environment (using sensors).
  • Think or make decisions (using its processor).
  • Act on its environment (using motors or actuators)

Examples of Robots

  • Industrial robots used in car manufacturing
  • Medical robots for surgery
  • Cleaning robots (e.g. vacuun robots)
  • Agricultural robots for planting or harvesting
  • Delivery drones

Advantages of Robots

  • Work faster and more accurately than humans.
  • Can work in dangerous environments (e.g., deep sea, space).
  • Do not get tired - can work 24/7
  • Reducee human error.
  • Increase productivity and quality.

Disadvantages

  • High cost to by and maintain.
  • May cause unemployment (replace human workers).
  • Limited intelligence - can only do what they are progrmmed for.
  • Require expert programming and repair.
  • Risk of malfunction or accidents.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) in Robotics

Modern robots use Artificial Intelligence to:

  • Learn from experience
  • Recognize objects and speech
  • Make smart decisions
  • Move and act independently

Embedded Systems

Meaning of Embedded System

An Embedded System is a computer system built into another device to control or manage its functions.

It combines:

  • Hardware (microcontroller, sensors, etc.)
  • Software (programs or instructions)

Definition

An embedded system is a small computer inside a larger machine, designed to perform a specific task.

Example

  • Microwave oven
  • ATM machine
  • Washing machine
  • Smartphone
  • Traffic light controller
  • Television remote

Characteristics of Embedded Systems

  • Dedicated Function: Performs one specific task only.
  • Real-Time Operation: Works and responds instantly.
  • Small Size: Compact and fits inside devices
  • Low Power Consumption: Uses minimal energy.
  • Reliability: Can run for long periods without failures

Advantages of Embedded Systems

    Highly reliable and efficient.
  • Performs tasks automatically.
  • Small and compact
  • Operates very fast.

Disadvantages

  • Hard to upgrade or modify.
  • Limited memory and performance.
  • Usually performs only one task.

Relationship Between Robotics and Embedded Systems

  • A robot contains one or more embedded system inside it.
  • The embedded system acts as the robot's controller, managing sensors and motors.
  • Without embedded systems, robots cannot process data or move inteligently
