Introduct to Computer

A Computer is an electronic device that accepts data(input),process it according to a set of instructions (program), stores the information, and the produces the results(output). Computers can perform millions of operations per second with great accuracy.

The word computer comes from the word "compute", which means "to calculate". However, modern computers do much more than just calculations - they can be used for communication, data processing, control systems, design, and entertaiment.

Examples of Computer Use

  • Education: Used in schools and universities for teaching and learning.
  • Health: Used in hospitals for patient records and medical research.
  • Banking: Used to manage accounts, ATM systems, and online transactions.
  • Business: Used for data management, marketing, and accounting.
  • Home: Used for entertaiment, communication, and online shopping

Characteristics of a Computer

  • Speed: A computer can process data and perform calculations much faster than a human.
  • Accuracy: Computer do not make mistakes unless the user enters wrong data or instructions.
  • Storage: They can store huge amounts of data and retrieve it quickly when needed.
  • Automation: Once instructed, computers can work automatically without human intervention
  • Multitasking: They can perform several tasks at the same time effeciently

Basic Function of a Computer

The computer performs four major operations known as the data processing cycle:

  1. Input: Entering data into the computer using devices like keyboard or mouse.
  2. Processing: The CPU interprets and processes the data using instructions from software.
  3. Output: The result is displayed through monitors, printers, or speakers.
  4. Storage: Data and results are saved for future use in storage devices.

Advantages of Using Computers

  • Increases productivity and saves time.
  • Improves accuracy and consistency of work.
  • Provides easy access to information and global communication.
  • Facilitates online education, e-commerce and remote work.

Limitations of Computers

  • They depend entirely on human instructions.
  • Cannot make decisions on their own.
  • Can be expensive to maintain and upgrade.
  • Susceptible to viruses and cyberattacks.
